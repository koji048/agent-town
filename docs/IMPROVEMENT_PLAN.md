# Office improvement plan — furniture, interior & landscape

Based on a study of office-furniture design and biophilic/landscape
principles, plus the owner's interview. Sources at the bottom.

## What the study says

**Furniture.** The modern direction is ergonomic and adaptable:
sit-stand desks, monitor arms (screens off the desk surface), task
lighting per station, benching systems with integrated dividers, and
matte, low-sheen natural materials (light wood, wool textiles) over
glossy finishes. Personalized ergonomic setups measurably reduce fatigue
and injuries; benching keeps collaboration density without cubicle walls.

**Landscape / biophilia.** Nature measurably improves focus (+6%
productivity, +15% creativity in cited studies): indoor-outdoor
connection through windows onto greenery, courtyards, natural materials,
plants at multiple scales, and "prospect and refuge" — open sightlines
plus sheltered nooks (a focus booth is exactly this).

## Owner's vision (interview)

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Style | **Scandinavian minimal** — light wood, white, clean, warm |
| Landscape | **Green courtyard** around the building, visible through windows |
| Signature elements | **Sit-stand desks + monitor arms**, **coffee bar/pantry**, **focus booth** |
| Accent (the 10%) | **Coral/orange** |

## The plan

### Phase 1 — Furniture & interior (Scandinavian + ergonomics)
1. **Workstation upgrade**: monitor arms (screens raised on slim steel
   poles), task lamps per pod, coral trim as the single accent line;
   pod dividers stay matte white.
2. **Coffee bar / pantry** (west wall): kitchen counter run with bar
   stools, upper cabinets, fridge, coffee machine — the social heart,
   on the studio-wing side of the circulation spine.
3. **Focus booth** (east wall): enclosed 1-person pod — dark matte
   shell, glass front, inner seat and mini desk. Prospect-and-refuge.
4. **Accent unification**: coral doormat, coral pod trims, coral wall
   art and meeting-room artwork; role identity stays on nameplates.

### Phase 2 — Landscape (green courtyard)
5. **Grass courtyard** replaces the gray ground plane; stone walkway
   leading from the entrance mat outward.
6. **Trees and bushes** placed outside the north and west walls so every
   window frames greenery (indoor-outdoor connection).
7. **Pond** with stone edge in the courtyard's south-east corner.
8. **Sky tint**: background from flat gray to soft daylight blue-green.

### Phase 3 — Polish (later)
9. Wool-look rugs in lounge, cushions with coral pops.
10. Reception brand panel with the office name.
11. Ceiling grid implied by aligned pendants over every zone.
