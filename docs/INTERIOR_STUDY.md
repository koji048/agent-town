# Interior design & space planning — study notes + self-critique

Applied study for the Agent Town office. Sources at the bottom.

## Concepts learned

**Space allocation.** A healthy office gives 40–50% of floor area to
workstations and keeps ~20% as clear circulation; the rest is support
(meeting, lounge, storage). Desks need ~90 cm front clearance; walking
routes need 90 cm (one person) to 150 cm (two).

**Circulation spine.** One primary route should connect the entrance, the
work zones and the meeting hub. Movement, sightlines and adjacencies hang
off this spine — a plan without one reads as furniture floating in a field.

**Zoning & adjacency.** Areas are organized by use (work / meet / lounge /
service), and an adjacency logic decides what sits next to what: reception
belongs at the entrance, lounge near reception (visitors), meeting room
near the director, focus work away from traffic.

**Focal point (emphasis).** One element anchors the room and draws the eye
first; everything else supports it. A room where nothing dominates reads
as inventory, not a place.

**Balance & rhythm.** Visual weight should be distributed — not all mass in
one corner. Rhythm comes from repetition on a consistent grid: aligned desk
pods, evenly spaced windows, repeated lighting.

**Scale & proportion, 60-30-10.** Furniture sized to the room; a dominant
color (~60%), a secondary (~30%), and ONE accent (~10%) — many competing
accents read as noise.

**Layered lighting.** Ambient + task + accent. A single uniform light
source flattens a space; light pools define zones.

## Critique of the current design (v. ab857cc)

| Principle | Finding | Severity |
|---|---|---|
| Space allocation | ~60% of the floor is empty tile; workstations ~25% | 🔴 |
| Circulation spine | None — open floor with no implied route between entrance, pods and wing | 🔴 |
| Adjacency | **No entrance exists**; reception floats mid-floor facing nothing | 🔴 |
| Focal point | Nothing dominates; the eye lands on the empty floor's brightest patch | 🟡 |
| Balance | All mass sits top-left (wood wing); bottom-right half nearly empty | 🟡 |
| Rhythm | Two pods offset diagonally, not on a grid; windows only on half the north wall, none on the west | 🟡 |
| 60-30-10 | White 60 ✓ oak 30 ✓ — but accents compete: 5 role colors + multicolor binders + green plants | 🟡 |
| Lighting | Single sun, uniform brightness; no zone-defining light pools | 🟡 |
| Landscape/setting | Diorama slab floats in a void; no ground plane to seat it | 🟢 |

## Improvements applied

1. **Entrance** at the south-east corner (doormat, flanking plants) — the
   reception counter moved and rotated to face it; lounge already adjacent.
2. **Circulation spine**: a darker tile runner from the entrance, along the
   pod field, through the partition door into the studio wing.
3. **Work pod grid**: four identical double-desk pods on a strict 2x2 grid
   filling the empty center — rhythm + space allocation in one move.
4. **Windows rebalanced**: continuous run on the north wall and three new
   windows on the west wall (daylight for the studio wing).
5. **Layered light**: pendant lamps with warm pools over the meeting table,
   reception and lounge.
6. **Seated diorama**: large soft ground plane under the slab.
